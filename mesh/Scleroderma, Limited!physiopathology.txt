The efficacy of self-administered stretching for finger joint motion in Japanese patients with systemic sclerosis.	To determine the efficacy of self-administered stretching of each finger in Japanese patients with systemic sclerosis (SSc).
Working ability in relation to disease severity, everyday occupations and well-being in women with limited systemic sclerosis.	To investigate how women with SSc and varying degrees of working ability differed regarding disease severity, everyday occupations and well-being. Working ability was operationalized according to the degree of sick leave.
